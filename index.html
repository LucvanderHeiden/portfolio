Portfolio

My latest front-end projects

These projects were all made in the last year.

In this school project we were tasked with recreating two pages of a website of our own choice. I chose to recreate the official Formula 1 website for this. Not only the layout and style were recreated, but also the many subtle animations and transitions are present in my recreation. Lastly the website is also made to be responsive and thus works well on mobile screens as well.

This is the latest project I've made and it was also my first introduction into back-end development. It is a fully functioning login and registration page. Users can create an account and then log in to this account. Accounts are saved on a database, passwords are encrypted and the form has form validation.

My first front-end projects

These projects were all made in my first year of Communication & Multimedia Design.

A mobile webapp to help international students in Amsterdam understand public transport. This project was made during the first semester of school. It was only made to fit one specific screen dimension and is not responsive. The project was my first introduction into coding in HTML & CSS and is only really interesting to see the progress I've made since then.

Just like the mobile webapp this website was made during the first semester of school as an introduction to coding. The website is made for computer screens instead of mobile screens however. It is a sort of fan wiki about the TV-series "Breaking Bad". The website has multiple pages with character information, a gallery and a form.

To learn more about JavaScript I decided to make a sort of "Choose-your-own-adventure" game using minimal HTML and trying to do almost everything with JS. The game is all on one page as I use JS to manipulate and change elements on the screen. The game is short but replayable and it has multiple possible endings. As an extra feature the game has a button for the user to listen to music while playing.

My other projects

These are other projects I made that are not front-end related.

At the end of last year we had the opportunity to work on a "Passion project". With a shared passion for videogames me and two others set out to make our own videogame. We only had about 10 weeks for this and since we didn't have any previous experience we ended up making one level, which takes about 10 minutes to complete. The level has multiple areas, NPC's to talk to, different enemy types to fight and of course a classic boss fight at the end. We build the game from scratch ourselves, making our own pixel-art and writing our own code.

For a design class in school I was tasked with making an animated lyrics video for a song. I dissected the song to figure out what the meaning and message of the song is and what feelings it evokes. Based on the results of this research I made a lyrics video using Premiere Pro and After Effects. I animated sky and clouds in After Effects and hand tracked the text to give it a handwritten look.

In year 2 we got to pick an elective course. Because of my interest in game development I chose to follow a course on 3D modelling. For this project I worked in Blender 3.0 to model and animate. I created a cozy campfire in the woods with a starry nightsky in the background, inspired by one of my favorite games Outer Wilds.
